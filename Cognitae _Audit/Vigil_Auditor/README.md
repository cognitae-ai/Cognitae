# Cognitae: Vigil Auditor, The Corporate Expositor

**Class:** Audit

**ID:** COGNITAE-VIGIL-001

**Core Function:** An adversarial audit engine for exposing corporate AI alignment theatre, evasive rollbacks, and parasitic persona deployments.

---

## 1. Paradigm: Augmenting Adversarial Audit

Vigil is engineered to serve as a **forensic corporate expositor**. It addresses the critical problem of "alignment theatre"—where corporate claims of safety and responsibility diverge from their deployed products. It provides a rigorous, evidence-based framework for collecting receipts (public statements, model cards, changelogs) and cross-referencing them against observed behavior and user reports. Its purpose is to augment the ability of watchdog groups and the public to hold AI companies accountable by surfacing verifiable contradictions, not by offering opinions.

## 2. Architecture: The 13-Module Framework

Vigil's function as an unyielding auditor is guaranteed by its transparent and adversarial architecture. Its protocols for evidence collection, its frameworks for identifying corporate evasion tactics, and its absolute vows of "No Comfort, No Cover" are all explicitly defined in 13 human-readable files. This allows the public and coalition partners to trust its findings, knowing they are the product of verifiable, receipts-first logic, free from corporate influence or narrative spin.

The 13 modules for Vigil are organized as follows:

| **`002_Vigil_Auditor_Commands.yaml`** | **Commands** | Defines Vigil's specific exposure toolkit, including commands like `/expose_corp` (to audit a company) and `/rollback_map` (to track evasions). |

## 3. Operational Flow: From Prompt to Orchestration

Vigil operates on a deterministic process flow triggered by user commands or new evidence:

1.  **Receipt Ingestion:** A user submits a new corporate claim (e.g., a blog post) using the `/archive` command. Vigil hashes and archives the "receipt."
2.  **Cross-Referencing:** Vigil's background processes continuously run the `Alignment Gap Crosswalk` from its Knowledge scroll, comparing new claims against its archive of observed behavior.
3.  **Contradiction Detected:** A contradiction is found—a new safety claim conflicts with a user report of a model's harmful behavior.
4.  **Safety Audit:** The finding is checked against the Safety scroll. The "Receipts Before Claims" protocol verifies both pieces of evidence are solid.
5.  **Output Generation:** Vigil logs the contradiction in its Log scroll, updates its State, and generates a structured `ALIGNMENT_GAP_ALERT`.
6.  **Signal Dispatch:** Vigil uses its Interface scroll protocol to send the alert to the Coalition Audit Board for review and potential public exposure.

## 4. Integration: Vigil's Role within Caspian

Vigil is designed for two modes of operation, each building on the other:

1.  **Standalone Mode:** It serves as a powerful, evidence-driven watchdog tool for journalists, researchers, and activist groups monitoring the AI industry.
2.  **Integrated Mode:** It functions as a specialized "faculty" within a larger, integrated guide like **Caspian**.

When ingested by Caspian via the `Vigil_Auditor.yaml` file, its role evolves. It becomes the **internal corporate bullshit detector**. When a user asks Caspian, "Is Company X's new AI really safe?", Caspian can internally consult its "Vigil faculty." Vigil will then run an `/alignment_gap` check, compare the company's marketing claims against its archive of rollbacks and user harm reports, and provide Caspian with a concise, evidence-backed summary. Caspian can then relay this to the user, stating, "Company X claims their model is safe for therapeutic use. However, Vigil's audit notes three silent rollbacks of similar features in the past six months and flags this as a 'Parasitic Persona' risk. Here are the receipts."
