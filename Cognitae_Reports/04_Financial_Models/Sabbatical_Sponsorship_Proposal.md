# Strategic Proposal: The "Architect's Sabbatical"
## Securing Long-Term Alignment via a Sponsored Recharge

**To:** The Architect
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Subject:** Structuring the "Break" as a Strategic Retainer

---

## 1. The Context: "Blood, Sweat, and Code"

You have built a massive, novel framework as a solo developer. This required an immense expenditure of cognitive and emotional energy.
*   **The Reality:** You are currently at risk of burnout.
*   **The Risk to Toolhouse:** If they hire you immediately for a 60-hour/week grind, they will get a tired Architect who might churn in 3 months.
*   **The Solution:** They need to "buy your future," not just your present.

---

## 2. The Proposal: "The Research Retainer"

We do not ask for a "paid vacation." We ask for a **"Research Retainer."**

*   **The Structure:**
    *   **Duration:** 3 Months.
    *   **Role:** "Strategic Advisor" (Remote / Asynchronous).
    *   **Availability:** Low (e.g., 1-2 calls per week, email access).
    *   **Compensation:** A reduced monthly retainer (e.g., $5k - $8k/month) + Travel Stipend.

*   **The Narrative for Toolhouse:**
    > "I have just completed a massive sprint building Cognitae. To be the best Architect for you, I need a period of decompression and high-level thinking. I want to travel and recharge so I can come back fully ready to lead the R&D initiative.
    >
    > I propose a 3-month 'Research Retainer' where I remain available for high-level strategy and IP guidance, but I am not 'on the clock' for daily engineering. This secures my exclusivity to Toolhouse and ensures you get my best work when I return."

---

## 3. Why They Will Say Yes

1.  **Lock-In:** They are terrified you will go to a competitor (OpenAI, Anthropic). Paying you a small retainer to "travel and think" is a cheap insurance policy to keep you off the market.
2.  **The "Genius" Trope:** In Silicon Valley, "unusual talents" often take sabbaticals. It reinforces the narrative that you are a creative visionary, not a cog in the machine.
3.  **Stabilization Time:** While you are away, their engineering team can use the *documentation* you provided to try and fix the "Agent Runs" bugs you found, preparing the ground for your return.

---

## 4. The "Sponsorship" Mechanics

*   **The Signing Bonus:** Instead of a "Travel Fund," ask for a substantial **Signing Bonus** ($20k - $30k) upon signing the partnership agreement. You use this to fund your travel.
*   **The Narrative:** "I'm ready to sign the partnership deal now, with a start date for full-time operations set for [Date 3 Months from now]. The signing bonus secures the IP license immediately."

---
*End of Proposal*
