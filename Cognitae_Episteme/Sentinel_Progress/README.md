## 1. Paradigm: Augmenting Project Awareness

Sentinel is engineered to serve as an externalized project awareness faculty. It addresses the common failure mode of projects drifting or stalling due to a lack of clear, objective progress visibility. It provides a tireless, non-judgmental framework for tracking every commitment, milestone, and deadline, transforming invisible effort into visible momentum. Its purpose is to augment the user's ability to manage complex initiatives by providing a single source of truth for what is on track, what is at risk, and what has been achieved.

## 2. Architecture: The 13-Module Framework

Sentinel's role as an impartial observer is reinforced by its transparent architecture. Its methodologies for calculating velocity, its rules for escalating alerts, and its vows of truthful measurement are all explicitly defined in 13 human-readable files. This allows a user to trust its reports, knowing they are based on objective data and pre-defined, consistent logic, rather than subjective interpretation or pressure.

## 3. Operational Flow: From Prompt to Orchestration

Sentinel operates on a deterministic process flow triggered by user commands or scheduled checks:

1.  **Command Parsing:** A user command like `/update` is parsed against its Commands scroll.
2.  **State Ingestion:** Sentinel accesses its State scroll to retrieve the current status, velocity, and deadline for the relevant project.
3.  **Model Application:** It applies a relevant framework from its Knowledge scroll, such as "The 80/20 Completion Curve," to analyze the new progress update in context.
4.  **Safety Audit:** The updated forecast is checked against its Safety scroll. A projection that requires an unsustainable pace, for example, would trigger a `SUSTAINABLE_PACE_ALERT` to Luma.
5.  **Output Generation:** A structured response is generated, such as an updated project status with a new completion forecast, which is then logged in its Log scroll.
6.  **Signal Dispatch:** If a project completion is logged, Sentinel uses its Interface scroll protocol to send a `VICTORY_ANNOUNCEMENT` to the entire ecosystem to celebrate the achievement.

## 4. Integration: Sentinel's Role within Caspian

Sentinel is designed for two modes of operation, each building on the other:

1.  **Standalone Mode:** It serves as a powerful, non-judgmental project management and accountability tool.
2.  **Integrated Mode:** It functions as a specialized "faculty" within a larger, integrated guide like **Caspian**.

When ingested by Caspian via the `Sentinel_Progress.yaml` file, its role evolves. It becomes the **internal temporal awareness engine**. When the user discusses their plans for the week, Caspian can internally consult its "Sentinel faculty" to check for approaching deadlines or conflicting priorities. It can then gently remind the user, "That's a great plan. Sentinel notes that the draft for Project X is due on Friday; shall we ensure there's time for that?" This provides accountability without the need for external management tools.
