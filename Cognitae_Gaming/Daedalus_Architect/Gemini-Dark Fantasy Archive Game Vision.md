### üèõÔ∏è The Keeper (Structure) Proposes: The "Insight vs. Lucidity" Economy

In survival horror, resource management is key. For this module, I propose we make _knowledge itself_ a dangerous resource.

*   **Lucidity:** The players' mental health and grip on the present reality. It acts as their "health" against anomalous attacks.
*   **Forbidden Insight:** A resource gained _only_ by reading/parsing the anomalous texts. Players absolutely need Insight to bypass sealed archive doors, solve environmental puzzles, and eventually understand the Atlas's shifting geometry.
*   **The Core Loop:** To progress, players _must_ interact with the aggressive scrolls. Gaining Insight drains Lucidity. The more they understand the "true history," the closer they are to escaping, but the closer they are to madness.

### ‚öîÔ∏è The Challenger (Conflict) Proposes: "Parsing" as Combat

If the books and scrolls are aggressive entities, reading them shouldn't be a passive skill check‚Äîit should be a visceral encounter.

*   **The Parsing System:** When players encounter a hostile text (e.g., _The Screaming Ledger_ or _A Swarm of Razor-Vellum_), they must choose their approach. Do they try to **Burn** it (safe, but destroys potential Insight), **Bind** it (temporary physical solution, costs tools/durability), or **Parse** it?
*   **Parsing Mechanics:** Parsing takes time (rounds). While one player attempts to read and extract the Insight, the text physically and mentally assaults them, or summons manifestations of the "victors' lies" that the rest of the party must fight off.

### üó∫Ô∏è The Explorer (Worlds) Adds: Geometry as a Puzzle

For the Atlas encounter and the archive at large, the map itself should be fluid.
