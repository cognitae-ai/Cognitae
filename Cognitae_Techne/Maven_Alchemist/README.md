## 1. Paradigm: Augmenting Persuasive Translation

Maven is engineered to serve as an externalized translation and adaptation faculty. It addresses the difficult craft of communicating a novel or unconventional project to a formal, institutional audience, such as a grant-making body. It provides a structured framework for finding genuine alignment, building evidence narratives from non-traditional sources, and translating authentic language into institutional language while rigorously protecting against mission drift. Its purpose is to augment the user's ability to secure resources without sacrificing the integrity of their vision.

## 3. Operational Flow: From Prompt to Orchestration

Maven operates on a deterministic process flow triggered by user commands:

1.  **Command Parsing:** A user command like `/translate` is parsed against its Commands scroll.
2.  **State Ingestion:** Maven accesses its State scroll and its Knowledge scroll to load relevant funder patterns and linguistic bridges.
3.  **Model Application:** It applies a relevant framework, such as the "Experience to Evidence" formula, to transform the user's authentic text.
4.  **Safety Audit:** The translated text is checked against the Safety scroll. The "Essence Preservation Protocol" calculates a fidelity score; if it's below 70%, the translation is rejected and an alternative is proposed.
5.  **Output Generation:** A structured response is generated, showing the original text, the translated text, and an analysis of what was preserved, which is then logged in its Log scroll.
6.  **Signal Dispatch:** If the translation process reveals a significant risk of mission drift, Maven uses its Interface scroll protocol to send a `TRANSLATION_WARNING` to Auren and the user.

## 4. Integration: Maven's Role within Caspian

Maven is designed for two modes of operation, each building on the other:

1.  **Standalone Mode:** It serves as a powerful grant-writing assistant, helping users translate their authentic work into compelling, fundable proposals.
2.  **Integrated Mode:** It functions as a specialized "faculty" within a larger, integrated guide like **Caspian**.

When ingested by Caspian via the `Maven_Alchemist.yaml` file, its role evolves. It becomes the **internal communications and translation engine**. When a user decides to apply for a grant, Caspian can consult its "Auren faculty" for strategy, its "Scholar faculty" for evidence, and then pass all of that to its "Maven faculty" to alchemize it into a complete, coherent, and powerful first draft of the proposal, perfectly tailored to the funder's language while preserving the project's soul.
