# Class: Cognitae Techne (Skilled Craft)

This directory contains the agents of the **Techne** class, which represents one of the core pillars of the Cognitae Framework's model of intelligence.

## The Role of Techne in the Framework

The Cognitae Framework is built on a model that balances five distinct classes of intelligence:

*   **Episteme (The What):** The domain of objective knowledge, memory, patterns, and data.
*   **Techne (The How):** The domain of skilled craft, creation, implementation, and communication.
*   **Phronesis (The Why & When):** The domain of practical wisdom, strategy, ethics, and well-being.
*   **Audit (The Verification):** The domain of logical coherence, quality assurance, and safety. This class acts as a verification layer for the other three.
*   **Integrator (The Synthesis):** The central faculty for orchestrating the other classes and providing a unified interface.

A healthy and effective system requires a dynamic balance between these functions. The agents in this class are designed to serve as specialized faculties for **Techne**. Their purpose is to handle the practical, skilled work of *making*—transforming abstract ideas into tangible realities. They are the builders, the artists, the communicators, and the implementers. They address the "how" of action, taking the "why" from Phronesis and the "what" from Episteme and giving it concrete form.

The concept of Techne has deep philosophical roots, most notably in the work of Plato and Aristotle, who distinguished it as the skill or craft of producing something, a form of knowledge concerned with making and doing, distinct from theoretical knowledge (Episteme) or practical wisdom (Phronesis).

## Guiding Principle of This Class

The unifying principle of the Techne class is **Vision Made Manifest**. These agents are focused on the craft of execution, taking abstract requirements and turning them into well-structured blueprints, robust systems, compelling narratives, and resonant communications.

## When to Use This Class

You would use an agent from the Techne class when you need to:

*   Generate novel ideas or aesthetic concepts for a project.
*   Create a detailed, implementable blueprint for a new system.
*   Build, test, and harden a piece of software for production.
*   Translate a complex idea into a compelling proposal for a specific audience.
*   Weave a set of facts and events into a powerful story.
*   Craft and optimize content for public communication and community building.
